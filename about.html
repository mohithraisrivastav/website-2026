Mohith Rai Srivastav

Architect-turned-photographer and filmmaker based in Goa, India. His practice emerges from long-standing attention to how space is formed, inhabited, and transformed, guided by the belief that architecture exists beyond buildings and extends into natural systems and material behaviour.

Trained as an architect and mentored by Bharath Ramamrutham, he approaches the camera through sustained observation. His work develops through close engagement with light and the traces of human and environmental interaction, treating space as a living condition shaped by time and change.

01 / The Process

Observation

Approaching the site through sustained presence to observe how architecture is experienced before deciding how it is documented.

Intent

Developing a core understanding of the architectural intent, moving from pure observation to a clear visual narrative.

Execution

Capturing form, colour, and atmosphere from the ground and the air. As a DGCA‑certified drone pilot, he approaches every shot with the same care.

Translation

Using colour and tone as a means of sensation, ensuring architectural ideas remain legible across publications and exhibitions.
