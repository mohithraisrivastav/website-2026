What Corbusier Got Wrong

A respectful disagreement with the master, delivered from the steps of the Capitol Complex at six in the morning.

15 min readMohith Rai Srivastav

To say that Corbusier got something wrong is to risk sounding impertinent. I am a photographer who has spent twelve consecutive winters visiting the Capitol Complex, and what I want to say is small: he got the weather wrong.

A building has to survive its photographer. Chandigarh barely survives midday.— Field Note, December 2021

The argument

He designed for a climate of pure sunlight. But the buildings receive fog, winter haze, the long humid afternoons of the monsoon. And in those conditions, the buildings become more Interesting than the drawings ever imagined.

This is not a failure. This is the thing architecture always does: it exceeds its author.

— M.R.S.
