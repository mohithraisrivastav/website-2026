The Monsoon's Instruction

Forty days of Goan rain and the small curriculum it delivered, one storm at a time.

10 min readMohith Rai Srivastav

The monsoon is not a season. It is a pedagogy. In Goa, between the first week of June and the middle of September, the weather takes over the classroom.

One: you cannot wait it out.

The second year I gave up on waiting. I started working during the rain. The photographs immediately became calmer.

Two: wet surfaces are different surfaces.

A laterite wall is a different photographic subject when wet. The colour shifts. The porosity becomes visible.

Three: silence is louder.

The constant percussion of the rain creates, paradoxically, a kind of silence.

Four: patience is not a virtue. It is a prerequisite.

The monsoon will not adjust to your schedule.

— M.R.S.
